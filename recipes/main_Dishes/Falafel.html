Falafel

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices, and deep-fried into small patties or balls. It is often served in pita bread with salad and tahini sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Pita bread, for serving
  • Chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber, lettuce), for serving
  • Tahini sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Process until the mixture is finely ground but not pureed.
  3. Add the baking soda and pulse to combine.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a bowl, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  6. Shape the falafel mixture into small balls or patties.
  7. Carefully lower the falafel into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
  8. Remove the falafel with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  9. Serve the falafel in pita bread with chopped vegetables and tahini sauce.
