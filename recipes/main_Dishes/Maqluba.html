Maqluba

Maqluba is a traditional Levantine dish that translates literally as "upside-down". It is a pot of stewed meat, rice, and fried vegetables, which is flipped upside down when served.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups rice
  • 2 large eggplants, sliced
  • 2 large potatoes, sliced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil and fry the eggplant, potatoes, and cauliflower until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add more olive oil if needed and saut√© the chopped onion until translucent.
  4. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Season with turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  6. Layer the fried vegetables over the chicken in the pot.
  7. Spread the soaked and drained rice evenly over the vegetables.
  8. Pour the chicken broth over the rice. Ensure the broth covers the rice by about 1 cm. Add more water if needed.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  10. Remove the pot from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before flipping it onto a large serving platter.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy!
