Hummus

Hummus is a classic Middle Eastern dip made from cooked, mashed chickpeas blended with tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and garlic. It is often garnished with olive oil, paprika, and parsley.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or one 15-ounce can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (1 large lemon)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for serving
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons water
  • Dash of paprika, for serving
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine tahini and lemon juice and process for 1 minute, scraping the sides and bottom of the bowl as necessary.
  2. Add the minced garlic, olive oil, cumin, and a pinch of salt to the mixture. Process for 30 seconds, scraping the sides and bottom as necessary.
  3. Add the chickpeas to the food processor and process for 1 to 2 minutes, until the hummus is thick and smooth.
  4. While the food processor is running, slowly add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water until the desired consistency is reached.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt or lemon juice if needed.
  6. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with paprika and chopped parsley.
  7. Serve with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or your favorite snacks.
