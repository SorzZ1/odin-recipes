Mansaf

Mansaf is a traditional Levantine dish made of lamb cooked in a sauce of fermented dried yogurt and served with rice or bulgur. It is a popular dish in Palestine and Jordan.

Ingredients

  • 2 kg lamb, cut into large pieces
  • 4 cups Jameed (dried yogurt)
  • 4 cups rice
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley (optional)
  • Large flatbread (Shrak or Markook) for serving

Instructions

  1. Soak the Jameed in water overnight, then blend it with the soaking water until smooth.
  2. In a large pot, cook the lamb pieces with the chopped onion, turmeric, cardamom, salt, and pepper until the meat is browned.
  3. Add the blended Jameed mixture to the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer until the lamb is tender, about 2-3 hours.
  4. Cook the rice according to the package instructions.
  5. To serve, place the flatbread on a large serving dish. Spread the rice over the bread, then top with the lamb and Jameed sauce. Garnish with toasted pine nuts and chopped parsley if desired.
