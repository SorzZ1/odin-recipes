Qidreh

Qidreh is a traditional Palestinian rice dish cooked with lamb, chickpeas, and a special blend of spices, typically served during celebrations and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup chickpeas, cooked
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil for cooking
  • 1/2 cup toasted almonds or pine nuts for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil and saut√© the chopped onion until translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the lamb pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Season with cumin, coriander, cinnamon, allspice, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stir in the chickpeas and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the drained rice to the pot and stir to combine with the lamb and chickpeas.
  7. Pour the chicken broth over the rice mixture. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Remove the pot from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
  9. Garnish with toasted almonds or pine nuts before serving.
